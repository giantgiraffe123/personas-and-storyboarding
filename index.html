Personas and Storyboarding

For Users of Andrews' Coca Cola Machines

For this project, I observed users using an interface and conducted interviews in order to build a persona to represent the standard users of this interface and a storyboard for one of these personas. For my interface, I chose the Coca Cola Machines at Andrew's dining hall in Brown University, and I observed how users interacted with and used the interface. Observations

  • Users generally first get a cup from the cup stack next to the machine and then wait in line
  • When it is their turn to use the machine, first they place their cup down
  • Users then press buttons on the screen, first to select the category of drink and then to select the specific drink
  • Some users know exactly what they want, others hesitate for a moment while they browse the options
  • None of the people I observed used the filters at the bottom to filter for low-calorie or caffeine-free drinks
  • Many people had to press the buttons multiple times in order to select their option - they appeared frustrated and impatient.
  • Then once the drink was selected, they pressed the pour button to fill their cup.

Interview Questions

  1. Why are you using this machine today?
  2. How do you know what you want?
  3. Do you come with an idea of what you want or do you decide when you get here?
  4. How do you find the drink you want on the screen?
  5. How do you get that drink into your cup?
  6. How do you know what buttons to press?
  7. How do you know where to place your cup?
  8. How do you know what the drink options are?
  9. How do you know when a drink isn’t available?
  10. What are the drawbacks / annoying parts about this interface?
  11. What would you change / add if you could?
  12. How would you describe the organization of the drinks options?
  13. How would you find pink lemonade?

User Responses

  • Users noted that usually they go to the machine to get the same drink every time
  • Users described the layout of the drink options to be hierarchical, with at first all the options displayed on the main screen, and the different flavors of those options available once you press on the drink.
  • Users expressed frustration with the number of times they have to click the screen before their touch is registered, especially when they are in a rush or there is a line of people waiting.
  • Users noted that the flavors could be larger/more readable, especially for the sub flavors.
  • The layout of the machine is intuitive to new users - with buttons that are circular and look like they should be pressed to choose a flavor, and are gray if they cannot be pressed since the drink is gone. Even though there is no sign on where to place your cup, the large surface under the drink selection allows users to assume the drink will pour from above when they press the pour button, and that’s where the cup goes.

Personas!!!

This is a persona to represent students who are familiar with the machine. The persona faces the problem of the interface not responding immediately to touch and the pressure of the line behind them. This persona represents these users because it generally has an idea of what they want when they go to the machine, experiences disappointment when their favorites have run out, and experiences difficulty pressing the buttons on the interface’s screen.

This persona reflects new users of the machine - such as first-year students. This persona faces the problem of figuring out how the machine works for the first time and getting acquainted with the interface. They too face the problem of a laggy interactive screen on the interface. The persona represents the users because it captures their shared uncertainty at first about the interface and quick ability to pick up on how to use it intuitively.
